## CONFIRMED CURRENT SITUATION (from your intel)

### Smithfield Street Facts
- **24 vacant storefronts** on the corridor
- **$9M infrastructure overhaul** - THE KEY CATALYST
  - Start date: Q1 2026 (approximately)
  - Duration: 18 months
  - Improvements: Wider sidewalks (1-8 ft expansion), new landscaping, lighting, intersection bump-outs, new bus shelters
  - ~2,500 weekday bus boardings on corridor
  - Ties into Mellon Square Park restoration
  - Described as "generational investment"

### Downtown Housing Pipeline
- **~1,000 new residential units** planned for downtown
- **~1/3 will be affordable housing**
- Several Smithfield buildings targeted including:
  - Smithfield Lofts (46 units in old House Building)
  - Potential Point Park University dorm/mixed-use

### Recent State Investment
- **$600M Downtown Pittsburgh revitalization** announced by state
- Smithfield didn't get "top billing" but benefits from overall downtown momentum

### Recent PDP Investment
- **~$95K recently invested** by Pittsburgh Downtown Partnership in:
  - Pop-ups
  - Facade improvements  
  - Planters + public art
  - Business attraction grants

### Recent Retail Activity
- Target, Burlington, Five Below opened in Kaufmann's building (since 2022)
- Viridis (vegan restaurant) replacing Wiener World
- Foot traffic rebounding but shopping still lags behind events/food

### 3. **PA Main Street Matters Program**

**Covers:**
- Physical improvements to buildings
- Facade restoration
- Business district revitalization
- Technical assistance

**Timeline:** Usually annual application cycles
**Action:** Check dced.pa.gov/housing-and-development/main-street-matters/ for current cycle

### 4. **Historic Tax Credits (If 201 Smithfield qualifies)**
**Federal:** 20% tax credit for qualified rehabilitation expenses
**Pennsylvania:** 25% state tax credit
**Combined:** Can get 45% of renovation costs back in tax credits

**Requirements:**
- Building must be on National Register or in historic district
- Substantial rehabilitation (usually >building's adjusted basis)
- Must preserve historic character

**Action:** Research if 201 Smithfield is in a historic district; consult with Historic Review Commission

### 5. **Opportunity Zone Benefits**
**What:** Federal tax incentive for investments in designated low-income areas
**Check:** Is 201 Smithfield in a Pittsburgh Opportunity Zone?
**Benefits:** 
- Tax deferral/reduction for investors
- Could help attract capital for expansion or building improvements

**Action:** Look up Pittsburgh OZ map on dced.pa.gov/programs-funding/federal-funding-opportunities/qualified-opportunity-zones/
